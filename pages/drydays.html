About Dry Days

A dry day is a day when the sale of alcohol is prohibited. Dry days are observed on major national holidays and festivals, as well as on election days.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is a dry day?
A dry day is a specific day when the government prohibits the sale of alcohol in stores, clubs, and bars.
Why are dry days declared?
Dry days are declared to honor religious or national sentiments, or to prevent any untoward incidents during elections.
How can I check for dry days?
You can use our Dry Days Calendar to check for upcoming dry days in your city.
